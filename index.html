Do Scientists Directly or Indirectly Cite other Scientists by h-Index?

This plot shows how scientist cite each other by reputation.

A single arc stands for a citation.

Blue arcs mark scientists with higher reputation that cite scientists with a lower reputation and red arcs mark the opposite.

Reputation is here defined by the h-index of a scientist.

This metric is far from perfect, but has significance alone from being used frequently on many science databases.

If we only show the citations with a high difference between h-indices, we see more red than blue lines.

That means that scientists with a low h-index cite scientist with a high h-index more often than the other way around.

If we visualize the opposite: citations with a low difference between h-indices, we see that most of these cites mostly happen in the lower h-index spectrum.
